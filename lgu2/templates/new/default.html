Commencement orders

s. 25F in force at 23 May 2014 by S.I. 2014/1236, art. 2(1)(e)

The Transparency of Lobbying, Non-Party Campaigning and Trade Union Administration Act 2014 (Commencement and Transitional Provision No. 1) Order 2014, art. 2(1)(e)

  1. art. 2 Provisions coming into force
    1. (1) The following provisions of the Act, so far as not already in force, come into force on 23rd May 2014–
      1. (e) section 25F (interpretation);

Legislation text 25F

  1. (Editorial note: the following text is subject to an commencement order) I1 25F

    Interpretation

    1. (1) In this Part—
      • “consultant lobbyist” means a person who carries on the business of consultant lobbying;
      • (Editorial note: the following text is subject to an amendment) F1 “employee” means an individual who works under a contract of employment;
      • to “engage in lobbying” means to make a communication within section 2(3) on behalf of another person or persons; (Editorial note: end of text subject to that amendment)
      • “information notice” means a notice under section 9;
      • “the Minister” means the Secretary of State or the Lord President of the Council;
      • “payment”, “in return for payment” and references to receiving payment are to be read in accordance with paragraphs 5 to 7 of Schedule 1;
      • “penalty notice” means a notice under section 16;
      • “quarter” means each period of three months beginning with (Editorial note: the following text is subject to an amendment) F2 1 January, 1 April, 1 July or (Editorial note: end of text subject to that amendment) 1 October;
      • “registered person” means a person entered in the register of consultant lobbyists;
      • “the Tribunal” means—
        1. (a) the First-tier Tribunal, or
        2. (b) in any case where it is determined by or under Tribunal Procedure Rules that the appeal is to be heard by the Upper Tribunal, that Tribunal.
          1. (i) one or more particular registered parties,
          2. (ii) one or more registered parties who advocate (or do not advocate) particular policies or who otherwise fall within a particular category of such parties, or
          3. (iii) candidates who hold (or do not hold) particular opinions or who advocate (or do not advocate) particular policies or who otherwise fall within a particular category of candidates.”
    2. (4235) Section 85 of the Political Parties, Elections and Referendums Act 2000 (controlled expenditure by third parties) is amended in accordance with subsections (2) to (6). (Editorial note: end of text subject to that amendment)
    3. (Editorial note: the following text is subject to an amendment) F7949 (703C) Section 94 of that Act (limits on controlled expenditure by third parties) is amended in accordance with subsections (9) and (10).
    4. (703D) Where the Registrar is required or permitted to serve a notice on a person, this is to be effected—
      1. (a) if the person is a registered company (within the meaning of the Companies Act 2006), by sending it by post to the company’s registered office;
      2. (b) if the person is an individual, by delivering it in person, by sending it by post to the individual’s last known place of residence or business, or by leaving it at that place;
      3. (c) in any other case, by sending it by post to the last known main address of that person;
      (but this does not prevent the Registrar from sending additional copies of the notice by whatever means the Registrar considers appropriate). (Editorial note: end of text subject to that amendment)
    5. (704) In determining whether material can reasonably be regarded as intended to promote or procure electoral success
    6. (705) The reference to doing any of the things mentioned in that subsection includes doing so by prejudicing the electoral prospects (Editorial note: end of text subject to commencement order I1)
  2. (Editorial note: the following text is subject to an amendment) F7950 25G

    Meaning of "controlled expenditure" (Editorial note: end of text subject to that amendment)

    1. (1) Section 94 of the Political Parties, Elections and Referendums Act 2000 (limits on controlled expenditure by third parties) is amended in accordance with subsections (2) to (6).
    2. (2) In subsection (3), for paragraph (a) (but not the “and” after it) substitute—
      1. "(a) either—
        1. (i) during a regulated period, any controlled expenditure is incurred in a part of the United Kingdom by or on behalf of a third party in excess of the limit for that part of the United Kingdom mentioned in subsection (5), or
        2. (ii) during a regulated period in relation to which any limit is imposed by paragraph 3, 9, 10 or 11 of Schedule 10 (periods involving parliamentary general elections), any controlled expenditure is incurred in a particular parliamentary constituency by or on behalf of a third party in excess of the limit mentioned in subsection (5ZA),".

  3. 26 Arrangements between third parties notified to the Electoral Commission

    1. (1) Part 6 of the Political Parties, Elections and Referendums Act 2000 (controls relating to third party national election campaigns) is amended as follows.
    2. (2) References in subsection (6) to controlled expenditure incurred by or on behalf of the third party include controlled expenditure that is, by virtue of section 94(6), treated for the purposes of section 94 and Schedule 10 as so incurred.
